About Intel

Intel is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Santa Clara, California, that designs and manufactures microprocessors and other computer components like CPUs. Founded in 1968 by Gordon Moore and Robert Noyce, its name is a portmanteau of "integrated electronics". Intel's products include CPUs for personal computers, data center processors, and AI solutions, and the company trades on the NASDAQ under the ticker INTC.
